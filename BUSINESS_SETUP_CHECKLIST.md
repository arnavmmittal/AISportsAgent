## Week 1: Legal Entity Formation

### Option A: DIY Washington State LLC (Cheapest: $200)

- [ ] **Choose Business Name** (10 min)
  - Check availability: https://ccfs.sos.wa.gov/
  - Suggested names:
    - [ ] AI Sports Agent LLC
    - [ ] Mittal Sports Technology LLC
    - [ ] [Your Name] Mental Performance Solutions LLC
  - **Rule**: Must end with "LLC" and not be taken

- [ ] **Reserve Name** (Optional, $30)
  - URL: https://ccfs.sos.wa.gov/
  - Click "Business Services" → "Corporations & Charities Filing System" → "Name Reservation"
  - Holds name for 180 days while you prepare
  - **Skip this if forming immediately**

- [ ] **File LLC Formation** ($200)
  - URL: https://ccfs.sos.wa.gov/
  - Login/Create account
  - Click "File a Document" → "Limited Liability Company" → "Certificate of Formation"
  - Fill out:
    - [ ] LLC Name (from above)
    - [ ] Duration: Perpetual
    - [ ] Registered Agent: Yourself OR Northwest Registered Agent ($125/year)
    - [ ] Your Address (if you're registered agent) OR Northwest's address
    - [ ] Management: Member-Managed
    - [ ] Organizer: Your name and address
  - Pay $200 filing fee (credit card)
  - **Approval time**: 3-5 business days
  - **Download**: Certificate of Formation PDF (save this!)

- [ ] **Get Registered Agent Service** (Optional but recommended: $125/year)
  - Why: Receives legal documents, keeps your home address private
  - Options:
    - [ ] Northwest Registered Agent: https://www.northwestregisteredagent.com/ ($125/year)
    - [ ] Incfile: https://www.incfile.com/ ($119/year + upsells)
    - [ ] ZenBusiness: https://www.zenbusiness.com/ ($99/year + upsells)
  - **OR**: Be your own registered agent (free, but your address is public)

### Option B: Stripe Atlas (Easiest: $500 all-in) ⭐ RECOMMENDED

- [ ] **Sign Up for Stripe Atlas** ($500 one-time)
  - URL: https://stripe.com/atlas
  - Click "Apply for Atlas"
  - Fill out questionnaire (20 min):
    - [ ] Business name
    - [ ] Business description: "AI-powered sports psychology platform for collegiate athletes"
    - [ ] Industry: SaaS / EdTech
    - [ ] Will you raise funding? Yes (even if unsure)
    - [ ] Will you hire employees? Yes
  - Pay $500 (covers everything below)

- [ ] **What Stripe Atlas Does for You** (automatic):
  - [x] Forms Delaware C-Corp (better for investors than LLC)
  - [x] Gets EIN from IRS
  - [x] Files initial documents
  - [x] Issues stock certificates (10M shares, you own 100%)
  - [x] Opens Mercury or SVB business bank account
  - [x] Sets up Stripe payment processing
  - [x] Provides legal templates (contracts, NDAs, offer letters)
  - **Completion time**: 7-10 days

- [ ] **Stripe Atlas Post-Formation** (you do this):
  - [ ] Sign documents in DocuSign (arrives via email)
  - [ ] Activate Mercury bank account
  - [ ] Get Delaware franchise tax login (Stripe emails instructions)
  - [ ] File 83(b) election within 30 days (tax form - Stripe provides template)

**Decision Point**: Choose Option A (DIY $200) or Option B (Atlas $500)

## Week 1-2: Tax Setup

- [ ] **Get EIN (Employer Identification Number)** - FREE
  - Only if you did DIY LLC (Stripe Atlas does this automatically)
  - URL: https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online
  - Click "Apply Online Now"
  - Fill out form (10 min):
    - [ ] Legal structure: LLC
    - [ ] Reason: Banking purposes
    - [ ] LLC name and address
    - [ ] Your name as responsible party
  - **Result**: EIN issued immediately (9-digit number like 12-3456789)
  - **Download**: EIN confirmation letter PDF (save this!)

- [ ] **Open Business Bank Account**
  - **Option 1: Mercury** (best for startups, online-only)
    - URL: https://mercury.com/
    - Apply online (15 min)
    - Upload: Certificate of Formation + EIN letter
    - **No fees**, free checking
    - **Approval**: 1-3 business days

  - **Option 2: Brex** (if you want credit card too)
    - URL: https://brex.com/
    - Business checking + credit card
    - No personal guarantee required
    - **Setup**: 2-3 days

  - **Option 3: Chase Business** (brick-and-mortar)
    - Find local branch
    - Bring: Certificate of Formation, EIN letter, driver's license
    - **Fees**: $15/month (waived if $2K+ balance)
    - **Setup**: Same day

- [ ] **Initial Deposit**
  - [ ] Transfer $1,000-5,000 from personal to business account
  - Purpose: Pay for insurance, subscriptions, shows you're operational

- [ ] **Get Business Debit/Credit Card**
  - Most banks issue automatically with account
  - [ ] Set up in Apple Pay / Google Pay
  - [ ] Use ONLY for business expenses (critical for IRS)

- [ ] **Separate Personal and Business Finances** (CRITICAL)
  - [ ] Stop using personal cards for business expenses immediately
  - [ ] Reimburse yourself for past business expenses from business account
  - [ ] Keep receipts for everything over $75

## Week 2: Insurance (MOST IMPORTANT)

**Why This Matters**: Universities REQUIRE insurance certificates to sign contracts. This is non-negotiable.

- [ ] **Get Insurance Quotes** (1-2 hours)

  **Option 1: Embroker** (recommended for startups)
  - URL: https://www.embroker.com/
  - Fill out online form
  - Industry: SaaS / EdTech
  - Annual revenue: $0-100K
  - Get quote for all 3 policies below

  **Option 2: Next Insurance** (cheap, simple)
  - URL: https://www.nextinsurance.com/
  - Fill out form
  - Get instant quote

  **Option 3: Hiscox** (popular for tech)
  - URL: https://www.hiscox.com/
  - Request quote
  - More expensive but comprehensive

- [ ] **Policy 1: General Liability Insurance** ($500-800/year)
  - Coverage amount: $1,000,000 per occurrence, $2,000,000 aggregate
  - Covers: Bodily injury, property damage, advertising injury
  - **Why you need it**: University contract requirement

- [ ] **Policy 2: Professional Liability (E&O)** ($1,500-3,000/year) ⭐ CRITICAL
  - Coverage amount: $1,000,000 per claim, $2,000,000 aggregate
  - Covers: Errors in your software, negligence, breach of contract
  - **Why you need it**: If AI gives bad advice and athlete claims harm
  - **Example**: "AI told me to push through injury, now I'm hurt, I'm suing"

- [ ] **Policy 3: Cyber Liability Insurance** ($1,000-2,000/year) ⭐ CRITICAL
  - Coverage amount: $1,000,000
  - Covers: Data breaches, ransomware, GDPR/FERPA violations
  - **Why you need it**: You handle student mental health data
  - **Example**: Database breach, 500 athlete records leaked, lawsuit + fines

- [ ] **Optional: D&O Insurance** (skip until you have investors)
  - Directors & Officers insurance
  - Only needed if you have board members / outside investors
  - **Cost**: $1,000-2,000/year
  - **Skip for now**

- [ ] **Pay for Insurance** (Total: ~$3,000-5,000/year)
  - Most insurers allow monthly payments ($250-400/month)
  - Use business credit card

- [ ] **Download Certificates of Insurance** (COIs)
  - [ ] General Liability COI
  - [ ] Professional Liability COI
  - [ ] Cyber Liability COI
  - **Save PDFs** - you'll attach these to university contracts

- [ ] **Add University as "Additional Insured"** (when you get contract)
  - Request from insurance broker: "Please add [University Name] as Additional Insured"
  - They'll issue updated COI with university listed
  - Free to add, takes 1 day

## Week 2-3: Accounting & Tax Setup

- [ ] **Find a CPA (Certified Public Accountant)**

  **How to find**:
  - [ ] Ask other UW startup founders for referrals
  - [ ] Post in Seattle startup Slack groups
  - [ ] Search: "CPA for startups Seattle"
  - [ ] Try: Pilot.com, Bench.co (online bookkeeping + CPA)

  **Interview questions**:
  - Do you work with software startups?
  - Have you handled SaaS companies before?
  - What's your fee structure?
  - Do you do bookkeeping or just tax prep?
  - Can you help with S-Corp election?

  **Expected costs**:
  - Bookkeeping: $200-500/month (or DIY with QuickBooks)
  - Tax prep: $1,000-2,000/year
  - S-Corp election: $500-1,000 one-time

- [ ] **Set Up Accounting Software**

  **Option 1: QuickBooks Online** ($30/month)
  - Industry standard
  - Connects to bank account
  - Tracks income/expenses automatically
  - CPA can access directly

  **Option 2: Wave** (Free)
  - Free accounting + invoicing
  - Good for simple businesses
  - Upgrade to paid for payroll

  **Option 3: Pilot.com** ($500-1,200/month)
  - Full-service bookkeeping + CPA
  - They do EVERYTHING
  - Expensive but worth it if you hate accounting

- [ ] **Set Up Payroll** (if paying yourself a salary)
  - **Options**:
    - [ ] Gusto: $40/month + $6/person
    - [ ] Rippling: $35/month + $8/person
    - [ ] QuickBooks Payroll: $45/month + $5/person
  - **When to do this**: When you elect S-Corp status (at $80K+ revenue)
  - **Skip for now** if just taking owner's draws

- [ ] **Understand Tax Obligations**
  - [ ] **Quarterly estimated taxes**: Due April 15, June 15, Sept 15, Jan 15
  - [ ] **Annual tax return**: Form 1120-S (S-Corp) or Schedule C (sole proprietor LLC)
  - [ ] **State B&O tax** (Washington): Quarterly filings
  - [ ] **Sales tax**: Not applicable for SaaS (services exempt in WA)
  - **Your CPA will handle all of this**

- [ ] **Consider S-Corp Election** (once revenue > $80K/year)
  - Saves $7K-12K/year in self-employment taxes
  - File Form 2553 with IRS
  - Must pay yourself "reasonable salary" via payroll
  - **Wait until**: First university contract signed and you hit $80K revenue
  - **Your CPA will advise on this**

## Week 3: Legal Documents

- [ ] **Get Operating Agreement** (LLC only, skip if you used Stripe Atlas)

  **DIY Template**:
  - URL: https://www.rocketlawyer.com/
  - Search "Single-Member LLC Operating Agreement"
  - Free trial, then $40/month (cancel after)
  - Fill in: Your name, LLC name, ownership percentage (100%)
  - **Why you need it**: Banks often require it, shows you're legit

  **OR hire lawyer** ($500-1,000):
  - Find: Washington startup lawyer
  - Get custom Operating Agreement
  - Also review university contract (worth it)

- [ ] **Get Contract Templates**

  **What you need**:
  - [ ] **Master Service Agreement (MSA)** - Overarching terms with university
  - [ ] **Statement of Work (SOW)** - Specific deliverables, pricing, timeline
  - [ ] **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** - FERPA compliance, how you handle student data
  - [ ] **Business Associate Agreement (BAA)** - If university requires HIPAA compliance

  **Where to get**:
  - [ ] Stripe Atlas (if you used it) - includes all templates
  - [ ] Docusign Template Library - https://www.docusign.com/
  - [ ] Hire lawyer for custom templates ($1,500-3,000 one-time)

  **OR**: Let university use their template (they probably have one)
  - Universities have standard vendor agreements
  - Just get lawyer to REVIEW it ($500-1,000)
  - Don't sign without legal review (liability clauses are tricky)

- [ ] **Get Lawyer for Contract Review** (BEFORE signing $100K deal)

  **How to find**:
  - Ask other founders for referrals
  - Gunderson Dettmer, Cooley, Wilson Sonsini (expensive but top-tier)
  - Local Seattle tech lawyers (cheaper)

  **What they'll review**:
  - Liability caps (limit to contract value or $100K)
  - Indemnification clauses (make sure they're mutual)
  - IP ownership (you keep IP, university gets license)
  - Termination clauses (30-day notice, not at-will)
  - Payment terms (net-30, not net-60)
  - Auto-renewal terms (don't let them auto-renew at their discretion)

  **Cost**: $500-1,500 for contract review
  **Worth it**: Absolutely - one bad clause can cost you $100K+

## Week 4: University-Specific Requirements

- [ ] **FERPA Compliance Documentation**

  Universities will ask for proof you comply with FERPA (student privacy law):

  - [ ] **Data Security Policy** (1-2 pages)
    - Encryption: "All data encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)"
    - Access control: "Role-based access, MFA required"
    - Backups: "Daily backups, 30-day retention"
    - Incident response: "24-hour breach notification"
    - **Template**: Use your ProductionSecurityRunbook.md

  - [ ] **Privacy Policy** (for athletes)
    - What data you collect
    - How you use it
    - Who you share with (coaches, with consent)
    - How to delete data
    - **Template**: https://www.termsfeed.com/privacy-policy-generator/

  - [ ] **Terms of Service** (for athletes)
    - What the service does
    - User responsibilities
    - Disclaimers (not medical advice, not crisis hotline)
    - **Template**: https://www.termsfeed.com/terms-conditions-generator/

- [ ] **Security Questionnaire** (University will send)

  Common questions you'll be asked:
  - [ ] Do you encrypt data at rest? **Yes - Supabase AES-256**
  - [ ] Do you encrypt data in transit? **Yes - TLS 1.3**
  - [ ] Do you have backups? **Yes - daily, 30-day retention**
  - [ ] Do you have disaster recovery plan? **Yes - see ProductionSecurityRunbook.md**
  - [ ] Do you have penetration testing? **Planned post-launch**
  - [ ] Do you have SOC 2 certification? **Not yet (first-year startup)**
  - [ ] Where is data stored? **Supabase (AWS US-West-2), GDPR compliant**
  - [ ] Do you use subprocessors? **Yes - OpenAI, Supabase, Railway**
  - [ ] Do you have cyber insurance? **Yes - $1M coverage**

  **Tip**: Answer honestly, explain mitigations, show you take security seriously

- [ ] **W-9 Form** (Tax form for university)
  - Download: https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/fw9.pdf
  - Fill in: LLC name, EIN, address
  - Sign and date
  - **University needs this to pay you** (required for all vendors)

- [ ] **ACH / Direct Deposit Setup**
  - University will ask for banking info to pay you
  - Provide: Business bank routing number + account number
  - **Never** give personal bank account

- [ ] **Certificate of Good Standing** (if required)
  - Proves LLC is active and compliant
  - Get from: Washington Secretary of State
  - URL: https://ccfs.sos.wa.gov/
  - Cost: $20
  - Takes 3-5 days

## Contract Negotiation Checklist

When university sends you contract:

- [ ] **Review Key Terms**
  - [ ] **Pricing**: $100,000/year ($8,333/month)
  - [ ] **Payment terms**: Net-30 (you bill monthly, they pay within 30 days)
  - [ ] **Term**: 12 months, auto-renew for another 12 months
  - [ ] **Termination**: Either party can terminate with 30 days written notice
  - [ ] **Liability cap**: Limit your liability to 1x annual contract value ($100K)
  - [ ] **IP ownership**: You retain IP, university gets perpetual license
  - [ ] **Data ownership**: University owns athlete data, you're just processor
  - [ ] **Insurance requirements**: $1M general, $1M E&O, $1M cyber (you have this)
  - [ ] **SLA**: 99.5% uptime (reasonable for year 1)

- [ ] **Red Flags to Negotiate**
  - ❌ **Unlimited liability**: Change to capped at contract value
  - ❌ **At-will termination**: Change to 30-day notice
  - ❌ **IP transfer**: Change to license (you keep ownership)
  - ❌ **Net-60+ payment**: Negotiate to net-30
  - ❌ **Automatic price increases**: Lock in pricing for 3 years
  - ❌ **Non-compete clauses**: Limit to 1 year, specific geography

- [ ] **Have Lawyer Review** ($500-1,000)
  - Send contract to lawyer
  - Get redline version
  - Negotiate with university

- [ ] **Attach Documents**
  - [ ] Certificate of Insurance (General Liability)
  - [ ] Certificate of Insurance (Professional Liability)
  - [ ] Certificate of Insurance (Cyber Liability)
  - [ ] W-9 form
  - [ ] Certificate of Formation
  - [ ] Data Processing Agreement (if required)

- [ ] **Sign Contract**
  - Use DocuSign or AdobeSign (professional)
  - Save signed PDF
  - Send invoice immediately

- [ ] **Set Up Recurring Invoices**
  - Invoice university automatically on 1st of each month
  - QuickBooks/Wave can do this

- [ ] **Track Expenses**
  - Save ALL receipts (Supabase, Railway, OpenAI, etc.)
  - Categorize in QuickBooks
  - Helps CPA at tax time

- [ ] **Pay Yourself**
  - Transfer from business account to personal account
  - Label as "Owner's Draw" or "Distribution"

  - **Don't** just spend from business account on personal stuff

- [ ] **File Quarterly Taxes** (CPA will do this)
  - Estimated tax payments due 4x/year
  - Based on profit (revenue - expenses)

## Red Flags to Avoid

**Do NOT**:
- ❌ Sign contract personally (use LLC)
- ❌ Skip insurance (university won't approve)
- ❌ Skip lawyer review (one bad clause = $100K loss)
- ❌ Mix personal/business finances (IRS will pierce LLC veil)
- ❌ Promise 24/7 uptime (impossible, use 99.5%)
- ❌ Accept unlimited liability (cap at contract value)
- ❌ Transfer IP rights (license only)
- ❌ Sign non-compete (limits future customers)
